How to Reduce Assisted Living Costs

  1. Explore Medicaid waiver programs

    Check if South Carolina offers Medicaid waivers that cover assisted living. Apply early, as waitlists can be long.

  2. Apply for VA Aid & Attendance

    Veterans and surviving spouses should check eligibility for this benefit, which can provide substantial monthly financial support.

  3. Consider shared rooms

    Semi-private rooms cost significantly less than private rooms and may work well for some residents.

  4. Negotiate rates

    Many facilities are willing to negotiate rates, especially for long-term commitments or during lower-occupancy periods.

  5. Look at all-inclusive pricing

    Facilities with all-inclusive pricing may be more cost-effective than those that charge separately for each service.
