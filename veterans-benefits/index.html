Frequently Asked Questions

Aid and Attendance is an enhanced VA pension benefit for veterans (or surviving spouses) who need help with daily activities like bathing, dressing, or eating. Monthly benefits can exceed $2,000 for a single veteran and more for married veterans.

Most veterans who served on active duty and were not dishonorably discharged are eligible for VA health care. Priority is based on factors including service-connected disabilities, income, and other criteria. Enrollment is done through VA.gov or your local VA medical center.

Each state offers unique benefits for veterans that may include property tax exemptions, state veteran pensions, education benefits, employment preferences, hunting and fishing license discounts, and more. Check your state guide for specific programs.
