Understanding Home Health Care

Home health agencies are licensed providers that deliver skilled nursing, physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy, and other medical services directly in the patient’s home. These services allow seniors to receive professional medical care while remaining in the comfort and familiarity of their own residence. Home health care is particularly beneficial for individuals recovering from surgery or hospitalization, managing chronic conditions, or needing ongoing skilled nursing support. When selecting an agency, look for Medicare certification, the range of services offered, and patient satisfaction scores available through Medicare’s Home Health Compare tool.

Medicare covers home health services for eligible patients who are homebound and need intermittent skilled care as ordered by a physician. There are no copayments for Medicare-covered home health services, making it one of the most accessible benefits available to seniors.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does home health care cost?

For Medicare-eligible patients, home health care has no out-of-pocket cost for covered services. For private-pay patients, skilled home health visits typically cost $150–$250 per visit, while home health aide services average $25–$35 per hour. Costs vary by region, type of service, and frequency of visits needed.

How do I choose a home health agency?

Verify that the agency is Medicare-certified and licensed in your state. Check patient satisfaction scores and quality ratings on Medicare’s Care Compare website. Ask about the specific services offered, staff qualifications, availability for emergency visits, and how care plans are developed and updated with your physician.

Does Medicare cover home health services?

Yes, Medicare covers home health care if you are homebound, need intermittent skilled nursing or therapy services, and your doctor orders a plan of care. Medicare pays 100% of covered home health services with no deductible or copayment. However, Medicare does not cover 24-hour home care, meal delivery, or homemaker services that are solely personal care.
