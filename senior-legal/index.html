Frequently Asked Questions

Every senior should have: a will or trust, durable power of attorney (financial), healthcare power of attorney/healthcare proxy, advance directive or living will, and HIPAA authorization. These documents ensure your wishes are respected and someone you trust can act on your behalf.

An elder law attorney specializes in legal issues affecting older adults, including estate planning, Medicaid planning, long-term care, guardianship, elder abuse, and Social Security. The National Academy of Elder Law Attorneys (NAELA) can help you find one in your area.
