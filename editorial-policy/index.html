Editorial Policy & Standards

This page outlines the editorial standards and policies that govern all content published on Senior Benefits Care Finder. We are committed to accuracy, independence, and transparency in every guide we create.

Updated: March 9, 2026

Editorial Independence

All content on Senior Benefits Care Finder is created independently. Our editorial decisions — what topics to cover, what information to include, and how to present it — are made solely by our editorial team and are never influenced by advertising relationships, affiliate partnerships, or outside commercial interests.

Our editorial and commercial operations are kept strictly separate. Advertisers and partners have no input into the content of our guides, no advance review of articles before publication, and no ability to request changes to published content. This separation is fundamental to maintaining the trust of our readers.

If a guide includes information that could be perceived as favorable to an advertiser or affiliate partner, it is because the information is factually accurate and editorially relevant — not because of any commercial relationship.

Accuracy Standards

We hold ourselves to high accuracy standards because the topics we cover directly affect people's health and financial wellbeing. Our accuracy requirements include:

  • Official sources required: All factual claims about program eligibility, benefit amounts, costs, enrollment periods, and regulatory requirements must be sourced from official government agencies (such as CMS, SSA, or VA), established industry research organizations, or other authoritative sources.
  • Direct citations: Guides include references to the specific sources used. When we state a benefit amount, eligibility threshold, or program rule, readers should be able to trace that information back to an official source.
  • No unsourced claims: We do not publish specific data points — such as income limits, cost figures, or enrollment deadlines — without verifying them against a primary or authoritative secondary source.
  • Current-year data: We strive to reflect the most current program rules, benefit amounts, and eligibility criteria. Our guides clearly indicate when they were last updated.

Author Accountability

All content on Senior Benefits Care Finder is researched and written under the direction of Paul Paradis, our founder and primary author. Paul is responsible for the editorial direction, factual accuracy, and quality of every guide published on the site.

This accountability means there is a named individual standing behind our content — someone who takes personal responsibility for ensuring that the information we provide meets our editorial standards. Readers can learn more about Paul's background, expertise, and editorial philosophy on his author page.

Review Process

Our content goes through a structured review process designed to catch errors and ensure quality:

  • Source verification: Before publication, all new content undergoes source verification. Factual claims are checked against the primary sources cited in the guide.
  • Regular review cycle: Existing content is reviewed on a regular update cycle tied to annual program changes. Key review triggers include Medicare Open Enrollment periods, Social Security COLA announcements, annual Medicaid threshold adjustments, and state-level program changes.
  • Update documentation: When guides are updated, the update date is clearly displayed so readers know how current the information is.
  • Quality checks: In addition to factual accuracy, we review content for clarity, completeness, and usefulness. Our goal is to ensure that every guide is genuinely helpful to the seniors and caregivers who rely on it.

Corrections Policy

We take errors seriously. When a verified error is identified in any of our guides, we follow a clear corrections process:

  • Prompt correction: Verified factual errors are corrected as quickly as possible after they are identified. We do not wait for a scheduled review cycle to fix known errors.
  • Reader reports: We encourage readers to report any information they believe is inaccurate. Corrections can be submitted through our contact page or by emailing corrections@seniorbenefitsfinder.com. Please include the page URL and the specific information you believe is incorrect.
  • Correction notes: Significant corrections are noted on the affected pages so readers are aware that the information has been updated.
  • Investigation: All correction reports are investigated against primary sources before changes are made. We do not alter content based on unverified claims.

Advertising & Affiliate Disclosure

Senior Benefits Care Finder contains affiliate links and advertising. These commercial relationships help support the operation of this site and allow us to continue providing free access to our guides. However, they do not influence our editorial content in any way.

  • Affiliate links: Some links on our site are affiliate links, meaning we may earn a commission if you click through and take a qualifying action (such as requesting information or signing up for a service). These links are clearly identified where they appear.
  • No pay-for-placement: Advertisers and affiliate partners cannot pay for favorable coverage, higher rankings in our guides, or preferential placement in our content.
  • Disclosure: We disclose affiliate relationships in accordance with applicable guidelines. When a page contains affiliate links, this is noted on the page.
  • Editorial separation: Our editorial team makes content decisions independently of our commercial relationships. The presence or absence of an affiliate relationship has no bearing on whether or how we cover a topic.

Content Categories & YMYL Responsibility

The majority of our content falls under what is commonly known as "Your Money or Your Life" (YMYL) categories. These are topics that can directly affect a person's health, financial stability, or overall wellbeing. Specifically, we cover:

  • Healthcare benefits: Medicare, Medicaid, prescription assistance, veterans healthcare, and other programs that determine how people access medical care.
  • Financial assistance: Social Security, SSI, low-income programs, energy assistance, and other benefits that affect people's financial security.
  • Senior care: Assisted living, home care, long-term care, and other services that affect people's daily lives and safety.

Because these topics carry real consequences for our readers, we take extra care with accuracy, sourcing, and presentation. We clearly state that our content is for informational purposes only and consistently advise readers to consult with licensed professionals before making decisions about their benefits, care, or finances.

This is not a legal disclaimer designed to protect us — it is a genuine reflection of our belief that personalized advice should come from qualified professionals who understand your specific situation.
