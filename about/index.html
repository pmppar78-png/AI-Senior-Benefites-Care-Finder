About Senior Benefits Care Finder

Senior Benefits Care Finder is a comprehensive resource helping seniors and their families navigate government benefits, healthcare programs, and care options across all 50 states. We are committed to providing clear, accurate, and trustworthy information on the programs that matter most to older Americans and their caregivers.

Updated: March 9, 2026

Our Mission

Every year, millions of seniors miss out on benefits they are entitled to simply because the information is too difficult to find, too complicated to understand, or scattered across dozens of government websites. We created Senior Benefits Care Finder to change that.

Our mission is to give every senior and caregiver in the United States access to clear, accurate, and actionable information about the benefits and programs available to them. We cover all 50 states and provide state-specific guides that reflect local eligibility rules, income thresholds, and program details.

We believe that understanding your benefits should not require a law degree or hours of phone calls. Whether you are a senior exploring Medicare options for the first time, a family member researching assisted living costs, or a caregiver trying to coordinate veterans benefits, our guides are designed to give you the information you need to make informed decisions and ask the right questions when speaking with licensed professionals.

Meet Our Author — Paul Paradis

Senior Benefits Care Finder was created by Paul Paradis, a consumer advocate and researcher dedicated to making government benefits information accessible to everyone.

Paul's work is driven by personal experience helping family members navigate the complexities of Medicare, Medicaid, and Social Security. After spending countless hours deciphering eligibility rules, comparing plan options, and tracking down state-specific program details, he recognized that millions of families face the same frustrating experience.

That realization led to the creation of Senior Benefits Care Finder: a single, trusted resource where seniors and their families can find comprehensive, well-researched information without the confusion of government jargon or the pressure of enrollment-driven sales pitches.

Paul personally oversees the research, writing, and editorial direction of every guide on the site. His commitment to accuracy and transparency is reflected in our editorial standards, fact-checking policy, and research methodology.

What We Cover

Senior Benefits Care Finder provides in-depth guides and tools across the full spectrum of senior benefits, care options, and financial assistance programs. Our coverage areas include:

  • Medicare — Parts A, B, C, and D explained; enrollment periods; Medigap supplemental plans; Medicare Advantage comparisons; costs and premiums by state
  • Medicaid — State-by-state eligibility, income limits, asset thresholds, application processes, waiver programs, and dual-eligibility with Medicare
  • Assisted Living — Costs by state and city, payment options, Medicaid waiver coverage, what to look for in a facility, and licensing requirements
  • Social Security — Retirement benefits, disability benefits (SSDI), Supplemental Security Income (SSI), spousal and survivor benefits, COLA adjustments, and claiming strategies
  • Home Care — In-home care costs, Medicare and Medicaid coverage for home health services, hiring private caregivers, and state home care programs
  • Veterans Benefits — VA healthcare, Aid and Attendance pension, veteran-directed care, state veteran benefits, and survivor benefits
  • Prescription Assistance — Medicare Part D, Extra Help/Low-Income Subsidy, state pharmaceutical assistance programs, patient assistance programs, and discount drug cards
  • Low-Income Programs — SNAP, LIHEAP, Section 8 housing, Lifeline phone service, weatherization assistance, and other need-based programs for seniors
  • Long-Term Care — Nursing home costs, long-term care insurance, Medicaid spend-down rules, and planning strategies
  • Disability Benefits — SSDI and SSI eligibility, application process, appeals, and state disability programs
  • Senior Legal Resources — Elder law topics, advance directives, powers of attorney, guardianship, and legal aid resources
  • Provider Directory — Finding Medicare-certified providers, nursing homes, home health agencies, and hospice care near you
  • Comparison Tools — Side-by-side comparisons of care options, costs, and program benefits to help you evaluate your choices

Each topic area includes national overviews, state-specific guides, cost breakdowns, eligibility requirements, and practical next steps.

How We Work

Every guide on Senior Benefits Care Finder is built on a foundation of careful research, verification, and regular updates. Our process includes:

  • Primary source research: We draw information directly from official government agencies including the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS), the Social Security Administration (SSA), the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), and state health and human services departments.
  • Industry data integration: For cost data and market research, we reference the Genworth Cost of Care Survey, Bureau of Labor Statistics reports, and data from organizations like the National Council on Aging and the American Association for Long-Term Care Insurance.
  • Regular update cycles: We review and update our guides to reflect current-year program rules, benefit amounts, eligibility thresholds, and cost data. Our review cycles are tied to major annual events such as Medicare Open Enrollment, Social Security COLA announcements, and state Medicaid program changes.
  • Fact-checking process: All factual claims in our guides are verified against primary sources before publication. We maintain a dedicated fact-checking policy that governs how we verify and correct information.

Learn more about our approach on our How We Research page.

Our Editorial Standards

We hold ourselves to rigorous editorial standards because the topics we cover directly affect people's health, finances, and quality of life. Our standards are publicly documented and include:

  • Editorial Policy — Our commitment to editorial independence, accuracy, and accountability in every piece of content we publish.
  • Fact-Checking Policy — The specific procedures we follow to verify factual claims, correct errors, and maintain the accuracy of our guides over time.
  • Research Methodology — A detailed explanation of our primary sources, industry references, and update processes.

We welcome scrutiny of our standards. If you believe any information on our site is inaccurate, please let us know and we will investigate promptly.

Why Trust Us

Trust is earned, not claimed. Here is what sets Senior Benefits Care Finder apart:

  • Editorial independence: Our editorial content is created independently. Advertising and affiliate partnerships do not influence what we write, what we recommend, or how we present information. Our editorial and commercial operations are kept separate.
  • Transparent funding: We are upfront about how we sustain this resource. The site may contain affiliate links and advertising, and these relationships are clearly disclosed. They never influence our editorial content or recommendations.
  • Cited sources: Our guides cite official government sources, established industry research, and reputable organizations. We do not make unsourced claims about eligibility, costs, or benefits.
  • No plan enrollment: We do not sell insurance, enroll users in any program, or recommend specific plans. Our role is to provide information that helps you make informed decisions when working with licensed professionals.
  • Corrections and accountability: When we get something wrong, we correct it. Our fact-checking policy includes a clear corrections process, and we encourage readers to report any inaccuracies they find.
  • YMYL responsibility: We recognize that our content falls under "Your Money or Your Life" categories. We take extra care with accuracy because the topics we cover — healthcare benefits, financial assistance, senior care — directly affect people's health and financial wellbeing.
