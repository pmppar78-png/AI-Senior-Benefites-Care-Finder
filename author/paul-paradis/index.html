About Paul

I'm Paul Paradis, and I built Senior Benefits Care Finder because my family needed it and couldn't find anything like it.

A few years back I watched relatives try to figure out Medicare enrollment, Medicaid asset limits, and what in the world "aid and attendance" means for a veteran's surviving spouse. The official pages were accurate but cold. The agent-run sites were glossy but obviously trying to sell something. Forums were full of outdated guesses. I spent weekends piecing together real answers from CMS, SSA, VA, and state Medicaid agencies, and writing them up in normal English so my own family could act on them.

This site is the grown-up version of those notes. I'm not a doctor, lawyer, insurance agent, CFP, or government official. I'm a regular person who cares about this, reads the primary sources, and tries to put the useful parts in front of the people who need them.

Mission & Approach

The mission here is simple: help seniors, veterans, and family caregivers understand the benefits and care options available to them, without talking down to them and without trying to funnel them to a sales call.

In practice that means three things. First, start with primary sources: Medicare.gov, SSA.gov, VA.gov, state Medicaid sites, the Genworth cost of care survey, and peer-reviewed research where it exists. Second, rewrite what those sources say in language a tired 67-year-old or a stressed-out daughter can actually read at 11 p.m. Third, be upfront about what the site is and isn't, and point people to the right agency or licensed professional when their question goes past general education.

The site uses AI tools to help draft, compare, and organize content across states and topics. Every page is published under my name, and if something is wrong, that's on me to fix.

Topics Paul Covers

The guides here focus on the programs and decisions that come up most often for seniors and their families:

  • Medicare – Parts A, B, C, and D; Medicare Advantage vs. Original Medicare; Medigap; enrollment periods; what things actually cost
  • Medicaid – Eligibility by state, income and asset limits, long-term care coverage, waivers, and what the application really looks like
  • Social Security – Retirement, SSDI, SSI, survivor benefits, and when it makes sense to claim early vs. wait
  • Assisted Living & Home Care – Typical costs by state, how people actually pay for care, Medicaid waivers, and how to evaluate a facility or agency
  • Veterans Benefits – VA health care, Aid and Attendance pension, VA disability, and state-level veteran programs
  • Prescription Assistance – Part D, Extra Help / Low-Income Subsidy, state pharmaceutical assistance, and manufacturer programs
  • Low-Income & Safety-Net Programs – SNAP for seniors, LIHEAP, Section 8, Lifeline, and other support most people don't know they qualify for

How We Work

Senior Benefits Care Finder is a small independent publication. One person's name is on the door, and the workflow is straightforward: read the primary source, write the plain-English version, link back to the official page, and update when the rules change.

A few ground rules I hold myself to:

  • Government programs get cited from government sources, not blog chains.
  • Dollar figures get dated so readers know how fresh they are.
  • If a topic needs a licensed professional (enrollment, legal filings, a Medicaid application review), I say so and point to one.
  • Affiliate relationships are disclosed. Nobody pays to change what I write in a guide.
