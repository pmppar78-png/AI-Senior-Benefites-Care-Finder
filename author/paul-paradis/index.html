Paul Paradis

Senior Benefits Advocate & Consumer Researcher at Senior Benefits Care Finder

Updated: March 9, 2026 Author Profile

About Paul

Paul Paradis is a consumer advocate and researcher dedicated to helping seniors and their families navigate the complex landscape of government benefits, healthcare programs, and care options. His work focuses on translating complicated eligibility rules, enrollment procedures, and program details into clear, actionable guidance that seniors and caregivers can trust.

Paul's interest in senior benefits advocacy grew from personal experience helping family members understand their Medicare, Medicaid, and Social Security options. He saw firsthand how confusing and overwhelming the process can be, and recognized the need for a reliable, independent resource that puts seniors' interests first.

Research & Editorial Leadership

Through Senior Benefits Care Finder, Paul leads the research and editorial direction of the site's state-by-state guides covering Medicare plans, Medicaid eligibility, assisted living costs, veterans benefits, and dozens of other programs. Every guide is built on primary sources from government agencies such as CMS, SSA, and the VA, supplemented by industry data from organizations like the Genworth Cost of Care Survey.

Paul believes that access to clear, accurate information is a fundamental right -- especially for those navigating complex systems during vulnerable times. He is committed to maintaining editorial independence, transparent sourcing, and a respectful, senior-first approach in all content on the site.

Topics Paul Covers

Paul researches and writes about a wide range of programs and topics affecting seniors and their caregivers, including:

  • Medicare -- Parts A, B, C, and D; Medicare Advantage; Medigap; enrollment periods; costs and coverage
  • Medicaid -- Eligibility by state, income and asset limits, application processes, waivers, and long-term care coverage
  • Social Security -- Retirement benefits, disability (SSDI), Supplemental Security Income (SSI), survivor benefits, and claiming strategies
  • Assisted Living -- Costs by state and city, payment options, Medicaid waivers for assisted living, and how to evaluate facilities
  • Home Care -- In-home care costs, home health aide services, Medicaid home care programs, and VA aid and attendance
  • Veterans Benefits -- VA health care, Aid and Attendance pension, VA disability compensation, and state veteran benefits
  • Prescription Assistance -- Medicare Part D, Extra Help/Low-Income Subsidy (LIS), state pharmaceutical assistance programs, and patient assistance programs
  • Low-Income Programs -- SNAP, LIHEAP, Section 8 housing, Lifeline phone service, and other safety-net programs for seniors
